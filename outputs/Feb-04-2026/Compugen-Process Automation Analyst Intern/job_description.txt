 Position Overview: 

Our Integrated Service Solutions team is seeking a Process Automation Analyst Intern that will document critical team processes, identify operational bottlenecks, and implement low-risk automations using existing platforms such as Microsoft Power Automate, ServiceNow, and Power BI.

Please note: This role will be full-time hours (37.5 hours per week), for a duration of 4 months (May 4 to August 21, 2026) with a rate of $22.00/hour. 


 Key Responsibilities:  

 Process discovery & mapping: Facilitate working sessions, capture inputs/outputs, handoffs, pain points; produce swimlanes, RACI and controls for priority workflows 
 Automation design & build: Configure production ‑ ready flows in Microsoft Power Automate and (potentially) task/workflow updates in ServiceNow
 Standardize patterns for intake to routing to status updates and notifications 
 Documentation & enablement: Create operating guides, runbooks, and guardrails so teams can own and extend automations post ‑ placement 
 Quality & compliance: Apply version control, testing plans, change records, and security/privacy checks; track outcomes against agreed success criteria 
 Data & insight enablement: Connect automations to Power BI datasets, define metrics (cycle time, rework, Service Level Agreement [SLA] adherence, throughput), and publish lightweight dashboards 

 Skills & Qualifications: 

 Must be comfortable with reading/writing workflow logic (no code/low code), Application Programming Interface (APIs)/webhooks, and basic data transformations 
 Exposure to: Power Automate, ServiceNow or Power BI (or similar tools) 
 Clear writing and visual communication skills with the ability to turn interviews into crisp process maps and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) 
 Curiosity and ownership: ability to test, measure, iterate, and ship value fast 
 Nice to have: SQL/DAX basics; Python for light data wrangling; experience with Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) tools; familiarity with Information Technology Infrastructure Library (ITIL) concepts (incidents, requests, changes, SLAs) 
 You must currently be a post-secondary student 
 You must be comfortable working in a hybrid work model with a combination of remote plus onsite work
