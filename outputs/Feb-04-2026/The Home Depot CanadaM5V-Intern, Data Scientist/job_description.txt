Over 16 weeks, you’ll work on projects that make a real impact, sharpen your skills, and build confidence for your future career.

Program highlights include:

Strategic Projects: Address genuine business challenges, explore innovative approaches, and contribute meaningful solutions.

# of positions available: 1

Locations: Askuity Office - 461 King Street West, Toronto. In office Tuesday – Thursday
Join Our Team:

Askuity is a Toronto-based retail analytics software company operating as an innovative division within The Home Depot. Our mission is to transform merchant–vendor collaboration by enabling AI-powered decision-making, predictive insights, and real-time retail execution. As a Data Scientist on the Askuity team, you will design, build, and deploy advanced analytics and machine learning solutions that drive measurable impact for The Home Depot and its suppliers.

Job Summary:

Our team is building Ask Fusion, a tool that makes it easier for suppliers to access key insights. As the platform grows, we need modular features that handle specific parts of the workflow reliably and at scale. This intern project supports that effort by helping us build one of these core components. The intern will build a small but important component of our Ask Fusion tool. Their work will focus on taking a specific user request, shaping it into the right format, and connecting it to our data systems. The work strengthens our analytics capabilities and reduces manual reporting effort.

What You Bring to the Role:

Currently pursuing a university and/or college education in Canada and enrolled in a co-op program focused on Computer Science, Mathematics, Statistics, or a related field.
Python programming (functions, modules, basic testing)
Experience with APIs or data workflows
Understanding of data structures and JSON
Basic familiarity with version control (Git)
Clear communication and willingness to ask questions
Ability to work independently and take ownership of tasks
Collaboration with technical and non-technical teammates
Problem-solving and attention to detail
GitHub (or similar) for code reviews
Python development environment (VS Code, PyCharm, etc.)
Optional but nice-to-have: familiarity with cloud platforms (e.g., Google Cloud), Postman, or Jupyter notebooks
The successful student will be currently enrolled in a Canadian accredited university or college throughout the period of the Internship/Co-op program.  In addition, you must be legally entitled to work in Canada by way of Canadian citizenship, permanent residency, or a valid study/work permit.

What you can expect to learn and gain from this experience:

Learn how to create reliable, production-ready modules that integrate seamlessly into existing workflows
