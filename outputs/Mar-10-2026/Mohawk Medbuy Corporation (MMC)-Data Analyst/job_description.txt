Position Description:	
Mohawk Medbuy Corporation (MMC) is a national, not-for-profit, shared services organization that supports hundreds of health care providers across Canada, as well as child welfare agencies and other public sector organizations. MMC provides value-driven contracting and procurement solutions for medical/surgical supplies, pharmaceutical products, local sourcing, capital (equipment, FF&E and redevelopment) and nutrition solutions. Other services include data analytics, in-hospital support, warehousing/logistics, technology, procure-to-pay, and accounts payable.

With an emerging focus on sustainability and reconciliation with Indigenous Peoples, MMC is actively supporting the creation of a resilient value chain that incorporates environmental, social and governance best practices. Based in Burlington, ON, the organization also has offices in Toronto, Thunder Bay, Kingston, Brantford, London and Chatham, and a distribution centre in Oakville.



We are currently recruiting for Data Analyst/Scientist (Co-op) to support our Business Transformation/Data Analytics team.


Position Overview


Student to help us bridge the gap between our ERP system (e.g., SAP, Oracle, NetSuite) and our future AI capabilities. In this role, you will not just build dashboards; you will dive deep into source data to connect disparate data elements, clean, structure, and prepare our data for machine learning and AI initiatives. You will work with diverse datasets to drive actionable business insights while strengthening our data governance foundation.


Key Responsibilities

Data Integration & Connection: Extract and combine data from our ERP system and other source systems (e.g., Handhelds, Warehouse, etc) to create a unified data view.
Data Cleaning & Quality: Identify, document, and fix data inconsistencies, missing values, and corrupted data to ensure high-quality data, acting on the principle "garbage in = garbage out".
AI/ML Preparedness: Structure and label data, preparing datasets for future predictive modeling and machine learning applications.
Insights Generation: Analyze operational data to identify trends, patterns, and inefficiencies, providing actionable recommendations to business stakeholders.
Visualization: Develop interactive dashboards (e.g., Power BI/Tableau) to visualize complex data in an easy-to-understand way for leadership.
Documentation: Map data lineage (where data comes from and how it changes) to support long-term data governance.
